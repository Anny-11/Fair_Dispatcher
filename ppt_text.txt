Slide 2:  |  |  |  |  | Problem Statement |  | Existing delivery dispatch systems are designed primarily to be cost-effective, focusing on minimizing distance, fuel usage, and delivery time . These systems ignore driver workload balance, repeatedly assigning complex or urgent routes to the same drivers. Route assignment does not account for real delivery effort, such as package weight, traffic conditions, road quality, number of stops, or urgency. Medical and high-priority deliveries lack structured prioritization. Absence of workload tracking and transparency results in driver fatigue, dissatisfaction, and reduced efficiency . There is no mechanism to provide fairness, flexibility, or controlled overrides in route assignment.
Slide 3: A Fair Dispatch System is proposed that assigns routes based on actual delivery effort instead of only cost metrics. Each route is analyzed using the defined constraints and assigned a route rank: HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW , ensuring transparency. The system maintains a driver workload history to prevent repeated overload of the same drivers. Medical and urgent routes are automatically prioritized in assignment. Drivers may request limited route‑difficulty adjustments using a token‑based mechanism, which are treated as soft preferences. Token usage can be overridden by admin approval at worst cases, enabling controlled overrides with accountability. The solution ensures fairness, efficiency, transparency, and sustainability , making it suitable for real-world deployment . | Proposed Solution |  |  |
